Period Just Started.
Didn't Expect It?

Breathe. This is one of the most normal things that can happen. Here's exactly what to do.

  1. 1
    First — breathe.

    You are safe. This is normal. You are not the first person this has ever happened to, and you won't be the last. Nobody is looking at you. You've got this.

  2. 2
    Get to the bathroom.

    Ask to use the restroom. You don't have to explain why. "Can I use the bathroom?" is enough.

  3. 3
    No products? Toilet paper works.

    Fold a few layers and place it in your underwear. It's a temporary fix — but it WORKS. It'll hold you over until you can get what you actually need.

  4. 4
    Ask someone.

    School nurse, a teacher, a friend. Most people will help you — no judgment, no questions. You'd do the same for them.

  5. 5
    Need products delivered right now?

    We can get it to you — wherever you are.

The Change, Sis 💜

Nobody warned us. Nobody taught us. That ends here. If something feels off — your moods, your sleep, your mind — you're not losing it. You're shifting.

🧠
MENTAL HEALTH
It's Not Just "In Your Head"

Anxiety you can't explain. Sudden depression. Crying out of nowhere. Raging one minute, empty the next. This is real, and it has a name.

  • Mood swings & irritability
  • Anxiety & panic attacks
  • Depression & low mood
  • Brain fog & forgetfulness
  • Feeling unlike yourself
  • Sleep issues → mental exhaustion
🔥
PHYSICAL CHANGES
What Nobody Told You to Expect

Hot flashes at 3am. Periods that show up whenever they feel like it. A body that suddenly feels unfamiliar. You're not falling apart — you're in transition.

  • Hot flashes & night sweats
  • Irregular or skipped periods
  • Sleep disruptions
  • Weight & metabolism changes
  • Joint pain & fatigue
  • Heart palpitations
📅
KNOW YOUR PHASE
Know Where You Are

This isn't one moment — it's a journey. And it can start earlier than you think, even in your mid-30s.

Perimenopause The lead-up. Hormones fluctuating, cycles changing. Can last 4–10 years.
Menopause 12 months with no period. Usually between ages 45–55.
Postmenopause Life after. Symptoms ease for most. This chapter is yours.
